Reasoning for Complex Data (RECOD) lab

I have worked mainly with recognition in open-set scenarios, investigating methods that can be applied to general open-set problems [4] [11]. For a great part of 2019, I am in a short-time post-doctoral research at Politecnico di Milano in Italy investigating forensic problems in the context of open-set scenarios [1].

For academic background, consider the following. I have obtained the master’s degree [13] as well as doctor’s degree [12] through University of Campinas (UNICAMP) in Brazil investigating recognition in open-set scenarios. As extra works, I have some collaborations—also in machine learning—in medical problems [5] [6]. Through Federal University of Ouro Preto (UFOP) I had obtained the bachelor’s degree in Computer Science [14]. During the bachelors, I had worked mainly with digital image processing in the speciﬁc problem of vehicle license plate location [9] and I concluded my education studying the Haskell functional programming language for image processing [14].
