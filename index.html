I am a Ph.D. candidate at University of Campinas (UNICAMP), working mainly with recognition in open-set scenarios, investigating methods that can be applied to general open-set problems [2] [9]. I obtained the master’s degree through UNICAMP in open-set recognition [10]. During the masters, I was member of a project ﬁnanced by Samsung in which we have worked in medical problems as well [3] [4]. This project generated some patents for an open-set method [12] [13] and for a heart view classiﬁcation method [14]. Through Federal University of Ouro Preto (UFOP) I obtained the bachelor’s degree in Computer Science [11]. During the undergraduate, I worked mainly with digital image processing in the speciﬁc problem of vehicle license plate location [7] and I concluded my education studying the Haskell functional programming language for image processing [11]. I have worked at University of Colorado Colorado Springs (UCCS) as a research assistant investigating open-set recognition for one year—2016–2017.
