I work as a researcher at \gls{recod} of the \gls{ic} at \gls{unicamp} since mid-2024, previously as a postdoctoral researcher \pubsssssss{ref:LusquinoFilho2024}{ref:Castro2023}{ref:Werneck2022}{ref:SorianoVargas2021}{ref:Werneck2024}{ref:Ferreira2023}{ref:Goncalvez2022}{ref:LusquinoFilho2022}.

For a great part of 2019, I have been in a short-time postdoctoral research at \gls{polimi} in Italy investigating forensic problems in the context of open-set scenarios \pubs{ref:MendesJunior2019}{ref:MendesJunior2019b}.

I have obtained the M.Sc. \pub{ref:MendesJunior2014b} as well as Ph.D. degree \pub{ref:MendesJunior2018b} through \gls{unicamp} in Brazil --- with a 1-year internship at \gls{uccs} ---  investigating recognition in open-set scenarios \pubssss{ref:MendesJunior2022}{ref:Neira2018}{ref:MendesJunior2017}{ref:MendesJunior2014a}{ref:MendesJunior2014}.

Through \gls{ufop} I had obtained the bachelor's degree in Computer Science \pub{ref:MendesJunior2012}.
During the bachelors, I had worked mainly with digital image processing in the specific problem of vehicle license plate location \pubs{ref:MendesJunior2011}{ref:MendesJunior2010} and I concluded my education studying the Haskell functional programming language for image processing \pub{ref:MendesJunior2012}.

As extra work, I have some collaborations --- also in machine learning --- in medical problems \pubss{ref:Pazinato2016}{ref:Penatti2015}{ref:Penatti2014}, estimation of geolocation through images \pubs{ref:Li2015}{ref:Li2014}, and on the development of a framework for machine learning experiments \pub{ref:Werneck2018}.
